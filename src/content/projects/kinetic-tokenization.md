*"Intelligence begins with ethical motion."*

Kinetic confronts a question raised across my recent work: when a dancer's
movement is captured and used to train generative models, who owns the result?
The platform treats motion not as an extractable resource but as authored
cultural knowledge — embedding informed consent, attribution, and royalty
distribution into the data itself through blockchain provenance.

The pipeline moves from ethical data collection (consent before capture,
cultural context preserved) through computer-vision movement analysis, to
discrete movement tokens, to smart contracts that automate royalties and verify
consent for every downstream use across gaming, robotics, film, and healthcare.

Kinetic is both an artwork and a working prototype — a counter-proposal to the
extractive logics that currently govern motion economies, and a companion to
the *Kinethics* and *Resisting Capture* strands of my research on authorship,
ownership, and transparency in AI choreography.
